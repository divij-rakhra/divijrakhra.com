How We Got 3,000+ Signups For Our AI SaaS In 24 Hours

April 2026 · 7 min

We launched a Figma plugin with zero paid ads. 24 hours later: 3,000+ signups. Waitlist included designers from Google, HubSpot, Netflix, Spotify, and Amazon.

The design director of Zero asked for a purchase order to get it for 150 of their designers.

Here's how we did it.

Positioning: The Five Stages

Every market goes through five stages of positioning evolution.

Stage 1: Desired Outcome — First mover says "I'll help you lose weight." No competition. Pure demand capture.

Stage 2: Dramatized Promise — Competitors enter. You differentiate with "cheaper, better, faster." Example: "Lose 5kg in 30 days."

Stage 3: Unique Mechanism — The promise gets crowded. Introduce a method: "Lose weight through keto."

Stage 4: Expanded Mechanism — Double down: "Intermittent fasting plus keto."

Stage 5: Brand — Nike territory. Pure emotion and brand affinity.

For AI tools, most markets are at Stage 1 or 2. This means you can win with clear positioning and a unique mechanism.

Our product: A Figma plugin for AI-powered design iteration. Our positioning: Cursor for designers.

The Three Distribution Channels

Marketing boils down to three options:

1. Ads — Linear, predictable, requires budget.

2. Cold Outreach — Cheap, one-to-one, hits deliverability walls.

3. Content — Mass influence. One piece of content reaches everyone simultaneously.

We chose content. Specifically, viral content designed to amplify itself.

The AutoDM Playbook

You've seen these posts. "Comment 'DESIGN' and I'll send you the resource." They feel cringe. They also generate massive revenue.

Here's why they work:

The action required to get the incentive IS the engagement. You want the asset? You comment. Your comment drives reach. More people see the post. More comments. The cycle compounds.

Our results: 335K impressions. 2,500 comments. 1,200 likes on LinkedIn. On Twitter: 75K views, 600 likes.

The Post Structure

The Hook (2 lines) — Capture attention. Don't mention the product yet. Pure intrigue.

Social Proof — "I got messages from designers at Slack, Meta, Spotify, ClickUp, and Amazon."

The Dream Outcome — "We've integrated AI in a workflow that lets us ship 10x faster than the average designer."

Pain Agitation — "Devs have completely integrated Cursor. Designers are still manually iterating frames."

The Product Pitch — Your positioning statement.

Call to Action — "Comment 'AI' and I'll send you the beta access link."

Seeding the Reach

Most posts die because they never get initial momentum.

Solution: Gather 15-20 people. Teammates. Friends. Fellow community members. When you post, they like and comment immediately.

The algorithm sees engagement. It pushes the post further. The snowball starts rolling.

No network? Use paid boosters. $50 to an influencer for a retweet. Seed the reach artificially, then let the content do the work.

The Funnel

AutoDM post → DM with link → Landing page with email capture → Thank you page with referral loop → Email nurture sequence.

The thank you page is critical. We gamified it. Leaderboard for referrals. Incentives for top referrers.

Result: 750 additional signups came from referrals. 35% of our total waitlist came from people referring other people.

The Email Sequence

They signed up. Now what?

Send a welcome email. Then one email every 2-3 days. Each email addresses a single pain point your product solves.

Keep them warm. Keep them excited. By the time you launch, they're ready to convert.

What to Do Today

Pick an asset your audience wants. A swipe file. A template. A framework.

Write the AutoDM post using the structure above.

Gather your ball rollers.

Post.

Engage with every comment.

Let the algorithm do the rest.
