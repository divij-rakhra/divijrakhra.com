How to Get Your Brand Mentioned by ChatGPT

April 2026 · 8 min

In mid-2024, we started a strategy that took our daily search impressions from 200 to 10,000. More importantly, it got Bricx mentioned in AI search results.

Someone searches "best UX agencies for AI startups" in ChatGPT. Our brand appears. They click through. They book a call.

This is the playbook.

Why Search Platforms Beat Discovery Platforms

Most founders focus on discovery platforms. LinkedIn. Twitter. Instagram. The problem? They require constant feeding.

Post consistently for two years. Build 20,000 followers. Then stop for a month. Your reach drops to 5-10% of what it was.

Search platforms work differently. Create one strong asset that people search for every month, and you get consistent traffic without daily effort. The asset compounds while you sleep.

This is the fundamental shift. Discovery platforms demand your time forever. Search platforms demand your time once.

The 5 Stages of Prospect Awareness

Every potential customer falls into one of five stages:

1. Unaware — They don't know they have a problem.

2. Problem aware — They know the problem exists but not the solution.

3. Solution aware — They know solutions exist but not the specifics.

4. Product aware — They know about you specifically.

5. Most aware — They just need a final push to buy.

Here's the breakdown that matters: 3% are actively looking for your product. 10% are considering solutions. 87% don't care yet.

For early-stage startups, ignore the 87%. Focus on the 13% actively looking.

The Long-Tail Keyword Strategy

Short keywords like "best UX agencies" are saturated. Clutch. Yelp. Big players with big budgets. You won't rank.

Instead, add modifiers to create long-tail keywords.

"Best UX agencies" becomes "best UX agencies for Series A startups."

"Top design firms" becomes "top design firms specializing in fintech dashboards."

Each modifier creates a niche keyword with lower search volume but zero competition.

The math works like this: one seed keyword × five modifier categories × 100 variations each. Suddenly you have 500 articles covering ground no one else is fighting for.

Why This Works for AI Search

When people use ChatGPT or Claude, they provide context. They don't search "UX agencies." They search:

"I'm a Series B fintech company looking for a UX agency that specializes in complex dashboards and can deliver in 6 weeks."

AI tools pull from niche sources that match these specific queries. Your long-tail content gets picked up because it matches the specificity of the prompt.

This is the opportunity. Most companies are still optimizing for Google. Almost no one is optimizing for AI search.

How to Execute

Step 1: List your seed keywords. What would someone search to find your product category?

Step 2: Create modifier categories. Location. Company size. Tech stack. Industry. Use case.

Step 3: Generate 50-100 variations per seed keyword.

Step 4: Use ChatGPT plus a VA or tools like SEO Baby to scale production.

Step 5: Publish consistently. We put out 500 articles in 4 months.

Building Domain Authority

Content alone isn't enough. You need domain authority to rank.

Phase 1: Directory submissions. Submit to 200+ directories. Get 200 backlinks. Move your DR from 0 to 20.

Phase 2: Link exchanges. Find peers in your space. Link to them. They link to you. Move from 20 to 30.

Phase 3: Paid guest posts. $50-150 per post on relevant sites. Move from 30 to 40+.

Our DR went from 10 to 41. Our average ranking position improved from 37 to 17. Closer to page one. Closer to being picked up by AI.
