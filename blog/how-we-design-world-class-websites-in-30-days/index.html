How We Design World-Class Websites In 30 Days

April 2026 · 5 min

Most agencies take 3-6 months to deliver a website. We do it in 30 days. The output is better. The client is happier. The margin is higher.

Here's the system.

The Problem With Traditional Agency Timelines

The old model: Discovery → Strategy → Wireframes → Design → Development → QA → Launch. Each phase takes weeks. Each phase has feedback loops. Each feedback loop adds days.

The result: 4-6 months minimum. Client fatigue. Scope creep. Delayed launches.

Speed isn't about working faster. It's about eliminating what doesn't matter.

The 30-Day Framework

Days 1-3: Intensive Discovery

One deep session. Not multiple 30-minute calls. 2-3 hours with stakeholders. Extract every insight. Decision-makers in one room. Questions answered immediately. No back-and-forth email chains.

Days 4-7: Strategy + Wireframes

Strategy isn't a 20-page document. It's a positioning statement, a site map, and wireframes for key pages. That's it. Everything else is procrastination disguised as work.

Days 8-20: Design Sprints

Design homepage. Present. Get feedback. Iterate same day. Move to next page. Present. Iterate. No week-long review cycles. Feedback happens in hours, not days.

Days 21-27: Development

Use tools that match your speed. Framer for marketing sites. React/Next.js for products. Don't reinvent the wheel. Use component libraries. Focus on what matters: conversion, not custom animations.

Days 28-30: Launch + Polish

Deploy. Test on real devices. Fix bugs. Launch.

What We Eliminated

Long discovery documents — No one reads them. Insights live in the team's head, not a PDF.

Multiple revision rounds — One revision per page. If it takes more, the discovery was weak.

Custom everything — Use proven patterns. Your navigation doesn't need to be innovative. It needs to work.

Endless QA — Test on 3 devices: iPhone, desktop, one Android. Ship. Fix what breaks.

The Client Communication Protocol

Slow projects die on communication lag.

Rules:

1. All feedback in one Loom video, not scattered comments.

2. 24-hour turnaround on deliverables.

3. Weekly syncs, not daily.

4. Slack for quick questions. Loom for feedback. Email for approvals.

Why Speed Improves Quality

Counterintuitive truth: Faster projects have better outcomes.

Why? Momentum.

When a project drags, clients second-guess decisions. Stakeholders want to see new variants. The CEO's nephew has opinions. The original vision gets diluted.

Fast projects maintain creative momentum. The strategy from day 1 is still fresh on day 30. Everyone's aligned. The output is cohesive.

The Pricing Advantage

Speed lets you charge premium rates.

A $50k project delivered in 30 days is worth more than a $50k project delivered in 4 months. Time has value. Launch delays cost opportunities.

Speed is a feature. Price accordingly.

What To Do Today

Look at your current process. Find the slowest part. Ask: "Does this actually improve the output, or does it just feel like work?"

Cut what doesn't matter. Compress timelines. Raise prices.

Speed wins.
